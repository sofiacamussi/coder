CURRENT THESIS:

As a young activist, artist, historian and construction architect, my career reflects diverse roles. Graduating as a technical architect in 2015, I gained hands-on experience in habitational projects, project management, logistics, and material science. I'm now exploring these facets within new environmental paradigms, examining how science, technology, and advancements engage with our daily lives, construction, and design. Currently, I'm delving into transitional projects in collaboration with secondary and university students in Argentina. Arduino is my primary field of exploration, aiming to empower Latin American communities with grassroots technologies and excercising the exploration of technological sovereignty in Latin American territories.
